**Question:** What is Valentine's Day also known as?

**Answer:** Valentine's Day is also known as Saint Valentine's Day or the Feast of Saint Valentine.

---

**Question:** When is Valentine's Day celebrated?

**Answer:** Valentine's Day is celebrated on February 14 each year.

---

**Question:** What is the historical origin of Valentine's Day?

**Answer:** The holiday originated as a Christian feast day dedicated to Saint Valentine, a Christian martyr.

---

**Question:** Who was Saint Valentine of Rome?

**Answer:** Saint Valentine of Rome was a Christian martyr, known for ministering to Christians persecuted under the Roman Empire during the third century.

---

**Question:** What notable miracle is attributed to Saint Valentine?

**Answer:** Saint Valentine is said to have miraculously restored sight to the blind daughter of his jailer.

---

**Question:** What romantic legend is associated with Saint Valentine?

**Answer:** A legend suggests that Saint Valentine officiated over weddings for Christian soldiers who were prohibited from marrying by the Roman emperor.

---

**Question:** What tradition emerged from the legend of Saint Valentine sending a note?

**Answer:** The tradition of written valentine messages may have been influenced by the legend that Valentine sent a note to the jailer's daughter signed 'Your Valentine' before his execution.

---

**Question:** When did the association of Valentine's Day with romantic love begin?

**Answer:** The association began in the 14th and 15th centuries, in conjunction with the rise of courtly love.

---

**Question:** What were common Valentine's Day gifts in 18th century England?

**Answer:** Common gifts included flowers, confectioneries, and greeting cards known as 'valentines.'

---

**Question:** What symbols are commonly associated with Valentine's Day?

**Answer:** Symbols include heart shapes, doves, and Cupids.

---

**Question:** How did Valentine's Day become commercialized in the 19th century?

**Answer:** The exchange of handmade cards transitioned to commercially mass-produced greetings, contributing to its commercialization.

---

**Question:** What is a symbolic tradition in Italian Valentine's Day customs?

**Answer:** In Italy, Saint Valentine's keys are exchanged as tokens of romance and are believed to have talismanic purposes.

---

**Question:** How is Valentine's Day observed in Christian denominations?

**Answer:** It is observed in various forms across Christian denominations, such as in the Anglican Communion and the Lutheran Church.

---

**Question:** How did Valentine's Day customs spread globally?

**Answer:** Customs spread from early modern England through English-speaking territories, and by the late 20th and early 21st centuries, they were adopted in numerous countries worldwide.

---

**Question:** Which East Asian countries have significant celebrations for Valentine's Day?

**Answer:** Singapore, China, and South Korea are notable for their significant celebrations and expenditures on Valentine's Day gifts.
